Board Questions Decoded

Each simple board question implies a complex technical audit. Here's what the CIO should actually investigate.

1

"Are we protected from a ransomware attack?"

Technical Audit Checklist This Question Implies

■Endpoint Detection & Response (EDR) coverage — are all endpoints protected?
■Backup strategy — 3-2-1 rule? Air-gapped backups? Tested recovery?
■Network segmentation — can ransomware spread laterally?
■Email security — phishing simulation results? DMARC/SPF/DKIM?
■Incident response plan — tested in last 12 months? Tabletop exercises?
■Cyber insurance — coverage limits, exclusions, notification requirements
■Privileged access management — admin accounts secured with MFA?
■Patch cadence — critical patches applied within 72 hours?
2

"Why does IT cost so much when we just moved to the cloud?"

Technical Audit Checklist This Question Implies

■FinOps maturity — do you have cloud cost visibility by team/project?
■Rightsizing — are instances sized correctly or over-provisioned?
■Reserved instances / savings plans — are you using committed-use discounts?
■Dual-run costs — are you still paying for on-prem while running cloud?
■Shadow IT — are departments buying their own SaaS outside IT governance?
■Technical debt — how much spend goes to maintaining legacy integrations?
■Total cost comparison — did you account for networking, egress, support tiers?
■Run/Grow/Transform split — what % is innovation vs lights-on?
3

"How do we know our patient data is safe?"

Technical Audit Checklist This Question Implies

■Encryption — data at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3)?
■Access controls — role-based access, principle of least privilege?
■HIPAA compliance — most recent audit results, any open findings?
■Data Loss Prevention (DLP) — tools to prevent unauthorized data sharing?
■Audit logging — who accessed what data, when, from where?
■Third-party risk — do vendors with PHI access meet BAA requirements?
■Penetration testing — when was the last external pen test?
■Data classification — do you know where all PHI/PII lives?
4

"What's our AI strategy and how does it compare to competitors?"

Technical Audit Checklist This Question Implies

■AI governance framework — policies for responsible AI use?
■Use case inventory — which AI initiatives are in production vs POC?
■Data readiness — is your data clean, labeled, and accessible for AI/ML?
■Talent — do you have ML engineers, or relying entirely on vendors?
■Competitive benchmarking — what are peer health systems deploying?
■ROI measurement — how do you quantify AI impact on outcomes?
■Regulatory compliance — FDA clearance for clinical AI tools?
■Bias & fairness — testing for demographic bias in AI models?
5

"If our main system goes down, how long until we're back up?"

Technical Audit Checklist This Question Implies

■RTO/RPO definitions — what are the targets for each critical system?
■DR testing — when was the last full failover test? Results?
■Geo-redundancy — are critical systems replicated across regions?
■Dependency mapping — do you know all upstream/downstream dependencies?
■Communication plan — who gets notified, in what order, through what channels?
■Manual fallback — can clinical operations continue without IT systems?
■SLA tracking — actual uptime vs target for last 12 months?
■Chaos engineering — do you proactively test failure scenarios?

Reusable Communication Frameworks

Templates the CIO can use for future communications with any audience

B

Board Update Framework

1. Bottom Line Up Front (BLUF) — One sentence: what happened and what's the impact?

2. Risk Assessment — What's the business risk? Use traffic lights (Green/Amber/Red).

3. Financial Impact — Express in dollars, not technical metrics.

4. Action Taken — What did we do? Keep it to 1-2 sentences.

5. Board Decision Needed? — If yes, frame the options clearly. If no, say "Informational only."

$

CFO Communication Framework

1. Budget Status — Over/under budget with exact amounts and trend.

2. Cost Avoidance — What did we prevent from costing more?

3. Optimization Opportunities — Savings we can capture with specific actions.

4. Revenue Impact — Always state, even if $0.

5. Investment Ask — If you need approval, present options with ROI for each.

T

Team Communication Framework

1. Recognition — Start with what went well. Name names.

2. Context — Why this work matters to the business.

3. Technical Detail — Keep it — your team needs it.

4. Lessons Learned — What would we do differently?

5. Clear Action Items — Who, what, by when. No ambiguity.

The CIO's Rule of Three: Before sending any communication, ask: (1) Who is my audience? (2) What do they care about? (3) What do I need from them? If your update doesn't answer all three, rewrite it.
