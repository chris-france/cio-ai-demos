How It Works: Savings Through Consolidation, Not Cuts

Positions Consolidated (3 HD roles)

Three help desk analyst roles are replaced by AI chatbot handling L1/L2 tickets. Those three people are retrained into higher-value roles (AI training, workflow automation, tech experience). Their old line items ($165K) are eliminated; they receive new, higher-paying positions.

Everyone Gets a Raise

Every person who stays gets a pay bump ($7K-$15K) reflecting their expanded responsibilities. Total raise cost: $163K. This is an investment in retention and capability.

Net Savings: $442K/yr

Old structure: $2,060K. New structure: $1,618K (11 dedicated roles + 4 retrained roles redeployed to other departments). The retrained staff cost is absorbed by the departments that receive them, reducing IT's headcount cost by 21.5%.

The Takeaway for CIOs

AI handled 7 out of 10 emails automatically — password resets, software installs, known issues, onboarding, permissions, reporting, and vendor tickets. The 3 that went to a human were: a critical system outage (patient safety), a security incident (BEC attack), and a physical AV issue (executive meeting). This is the right split. One person reviewing AI-handled tickets replaces three people doing manual triage. The humans focus on what only humans can do.

Communication Plan Template

A phased communication strategy for announcing the IT restructure. Customize for your organization.

1

Leadership Alignment

Week 1-2

Audience: CEO, CFO, CHRO, fellow VPs

Channel: In-person executive briefing (1 hour)

Key Messages:

  • This is a transformation, not a reduction. Zero layoffs.
  • AI handles routine tasks; humans handle strategy, judgment, and relationships.
  • 21.5% cost savings ($442K/yr) while increasing team capability.
  • Every affected employee gets a raise and a higher-value role.
  • Training investment: ~$80K (certs, bootcamps, tools). ROI in <3 months.

Ask: Approval to proceed, HR partnership for role changes, budget for training.

2

Direct Reports & Managers

Week 3

Audience: IT Director, team leads (Sarah, James)

Channel: Private 1:1 meetings, then small group

Key Messages:

  • Share the full plan before the team hears anything. They need to be partners, not surprised.
  • Their roles expand — more strategic, more interesting, more valued.
  • They will help design the training plan for their teams.
  • Address concerns privately. Expect: "Will my team resent me?"

Deliverable: Manager FAQ document with answers to anticipated questions.

3

Individual Conversations

Week 4

Audience: Each team member individually (priority: the 4 being retrained)

Channel: Private 1:1 with their manager + HR present

Key Messages:

  • "Your job is not being eliminated. Your job is being upgraded."
  • Show their specific: new title, new salary (higher), new responsibilities, training plan.
  • "We're investing in you because you know this organization. That knowledge is irreplaceable."
  • Give them time to process. Schedule a follow-up in 48 hours.

Deliverable: Individual transition letter with new title, salary, timeline, and training details.

4

Full Team Announcement

Week 5

Audience: Entire IT team (all 15)

Channel: In-person all-hands meeting (CIO presents)

Key Messages:

  • Frame as: "We're building the IT team of the future — and every one of you is part of it."
  • Show the vision: current org → future org. Highlight everyone's new role.
  • Announce training budget, timeline, and support resources.
  • Open Q&A. Be transparent about what changes and what doesn't.

Tone: Confident but empathetic. Acknowledge the change is significant. Celebrate the opportunity.

5

Broader Organization

Week 6

Audience: All employees (IT's "customers")

Channel: Company-wide email + intranet post + town hall mention

Key Messages:

  • "IT is evolving to serve you better. Here's what changes for you."
  • New AI chatbot for instant help (password resets in seconds, not hours).
  • Same people you know and trust, now focused on bigger problems.
  • Faster response times, 24/7 AI support, human backup always available.

Deliverable: FAQ page on intranet, new IT contact guide, AI chatbot launch announcement.

What NOT To Do

x

Don't announce to everyone at once

People whose roles change most should hear first, privately, with time to process.

x

Don't use the word "replaced"

Use "evolved," "transformed," "upgraded." Language matters enormously.

x

Don't lead with cost savings

Lead with capability and career growth. Cost savings are for the CFO deck, not the team meeting.

x

Don't skip the follow-up

Check in weekly during months 1-3. Anxiety peaks after the announcement, not during.
