Key Findings
!
Talent strategy is critically under-funded. "Attract & Retain Talent" receives just $420K (0.4% of IT budget) despite being a board-approved priority. With 18,500 employees in a competitive healthcare labor market, this gap poses material execution risk to every other pillar.
!
Telehealth lacks infrastructure depth. Two clinical delivery projects ($3.4M) are well-funded, but neither addresses the connectivity and edge infrastructure needed to reach 50,000 additional rural patients. The board target requires infrastructure investment, not just application rollout.
!
1 orphan project detected. "Mobile App for Clinicians" ($1.2M) is mapped to "Reduce Burnout" — not a board-approved priority. Either re-map to "Modernize Legacy EHR" or seek board endorsement for a new priority. No project should be funded without strategic traceability.
✓
Transform allocation is aggressive and intentional. At 32% (vs. 20% industry norm), Meridian is signaling a serious modernization posture. Execution risk is elevated, but the portfolio is well-diversified across AI, cloud, and automation.
Recommendations for Board Consideration
Recommendation 1
Close the Talent Gap
Fund 3 new projects: AI-powered recruiting platform, learning management system, and employee experience analytics. Essential to support all other transformation initiatives.
Est. investment: $830K/yr
Recommendation 2
Add Rural Infrastructure
Commission a rural connectivity & edge compute project to support the 50K-patient telehealth expansion. Current projects deliver software but not the network reach needed for rural access.
Est. investment: $600K/yr
Recommendation 3
Resolve Orphan Project
Re-map the $1.2M "Mobile App for Clinicians" to the "Modernize Legacy EHR" pillar (clinician workflow improvement) or formally adopt "Reduce Burnout" as an 8th strategic priority.
No additional cost — governance action
